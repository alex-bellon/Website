About Me

My name is Alex Bellon, and I am currently a freshman Computer Science major at the University of Texas at Austin. I have been programming since 8th grade, and am most comfortable with Java and Python, although I am familiar with C/C++ and HTML. Besides projects for my courses, I love attending Hackathons with my team 'The Waitlisters', as well as participating in Capture the Flag competitions (CTFs) through the Information & Systems Security Society here at UT.

While a lot of my time is dedicated to Computer Science, I also enjoy drawing, longboarding, competing in pub style trivia, seeing Broadway shows and playing soccer and Quidditch (yes, it's a real sport!). I have been making art for as long as I can remember, and one of my proudest moments was when one of my AP Studio Art pieces was awarded a Gold Key in the Scholastic Art & Writing Awards, and then chose as 1 of 30 pieces to be displayed at the U.S. Department of Education in Washington, D.C.!

I am also interested in foreign languages, a result of living abroad in Lugano, Switzerland for 5 years during elementary and middle school. In Switzerland I spent 5 years learning Italian, and when I moved back to the United States I spent 4 learning German. In high school I participated in the Texas State German Competition and consistently placed in the Top 3 at Regionals and Top 10 at State the 2 years I participated.
