Is the Lab Full?

Is the Lab Full? allows students to tweet to a Twitter account linked to one of UTCS' Building Wide Intelligence (BWI) robots. The robot will then go into the CS lab in the GDC and count the amount of people in the lab using the YOLO v3 algorithm. It would then reply with the number of people it counted and what percentage of the CS lab is full. This project was made as a final project for my CS309: Autonomous Intelligent Robots course. I worked on the Python and bash scripts that collected and sent tweets, as well as handled the image recognition and sending nav goals to the robot.

Crowdsourced Cartographic Catastrophe Analysis (CCCA)

CCCA uses the frequency of tweets with location enabled and keywords relevant to natural disasters to form a heatmap from which first responders and helpers can decide which areas are most in need of assistance. Placed 2nd at Texas Convergent Side Project Expo!

This project was made for TAMUhack 2018 by The Waitlisters. I wrote the Python script that received, analyzed and formatted tweets, then exported them to a .csv file.
