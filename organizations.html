Cybersecurity Organizations

CCDC

I am the Captain of UT's Collegiate Cybersecurity Defense Competition (CCDC) team. In CCDC, teams of 8 students must defend 8-10 machines from red team attackers while also completing business 'injects' (like setting up new services, managing users, etc.). As captain, I delegate tasks and work on any extra injects that need help. At the Southwest Regional we have placed 2nd ('19) and 3rd ('20) place.

CPTC

I am the Co-Captain of UT's Collegiate Penetration Testing Competition (CPTC) team. CPTC is a competition where teams of 6 students are tasked with performing a comprehensive penetration test for a fake company, and then delivering a detailed report of the vulnerabilities and security flaws they found. This past season, my team placed 2nd at the New England Regionals.

ISSS

I am the President of the Information & System Security Society, a club dedicated to cybersecurity. I write problems and challenges for the CTFs we hold every two weeks, and hold talks about introductory security topics. I manage an officer team of 15, and we have 100+ members. We also hold a yearly international CTF called UTCTF that has 1200+ competitors from all over the globe (I lead this project in 2020).

Computer Science Organizations

ACM

I am a member of UT's Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) chapter. I served as Webmaster for 2 years keeping the TexasACM.org website up to date and free of bugs. I helped respond to issues on our GitHub page, as well as maintain the GitHub Wiki. I also wrote curriculum and hosted a series of introductory workshops for freshmen to introduce them to topics like Linux, Git/VCS, debugging, and more.

Waitlisters

I am one of the co-founders of a hackathon team called 'The Waitlisters'. Started at HackTX 2017, we have since particpated in dozens of hackathons. We have multiple top 3 finishes, and we even took home the grand prize at TAMUhack 2019. Besides Hackathons, we also particpate in CTFs and Programming Competitions.
